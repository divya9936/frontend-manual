About the Creator

👨‍💻

Built from real production experience

I built Frontend Manual because I was frustrated with the gap between what courses teach and what actually matters in real development.

After shipping multiple React applications at scale, debugging production issues, and learning some hard lessons the painful way, I realized something important: most of the knowledge that saves time and prevents bugs never makes it into tutorials or courses.

This is my collection of patterns, gotchas, and practical wisdom from actually shipping code. It's the guide I wish I had when I started building for production.

Why This Exists

I got frustrated watching tutorials teach syntax and frameworks, but nobody teaches the wisdom. How do you think about component architecture? What hidden bugs should you be paranoid about? When do you optimize vs. just ship? These are the questions that experience answers—not courses.

After several years shipping products, I started collecting patterns. Not just code—but the thinking behind it. The edge cases. The gotchas nobody mentions. The tradeoffs you only learn by making mistakes.

This manual is that collection. It's meant to save you the years it took me to learn these lessons. Not to replace books or courses, but to be the practical guide for the stuff that actually matters.

This is what I wish I had when I started shipping code to production.
